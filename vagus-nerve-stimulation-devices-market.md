<p><p>Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS) devices are categorized into implantable and external devices. Implantable VNS devices are surgically placed under the skin and connected to the vagus nerve, delivering electrical impulses to manage conditions like epilepsy and depression. In contrast, external VNS devices are non-invasive, utilizing surface electrodes to stimulate the vagus nerve without surgical procedures. Both types aim to alleviate symptoms of neurological disorders, enhancing patient quality of life through targeted nerve stimulation therapies.</p></p>
