<p><p>Infrared spectroscopy encompasses three primary market types: Near-Infrared (NIR), Mid-Infrared (Mid IR), and Far-Infrared (Far IR). NIR spectroscopy, operating in the 0.75-2.5 µm range, is widely used in agricultural and pharmaceutical applications for rapid analysis. Mid IR, ranging from 2.5 to 25 µm, focuses on molecular vibrations, making it ideal for organic compounds and chemical analysis. Far IR, above 25 µm, is primarily utilized in solid-state physics and material science, providing insights into crystal structures and lattice dynamics.</p></p>
