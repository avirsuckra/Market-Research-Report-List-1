<p><p>The Low-E insulated glass market is categorized primarily into hard-coated and soft-coated types. Hard-coated Low-E glass features a durable, thin metallic layer applied during manufacturing, providing good thermal performance and resistance to abrasion. It is typically used in commercial applications. In contrast, soft-coated Low-E glass involves a delicate application of silver and requires a protective layer, offering superior energy efficiency and better performance in residential settings. Both types enhance insulation and reduce energy costs by minimizing heat transfer.</p></p>
