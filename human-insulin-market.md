<p><p>The human insulin market comprises primarily two categories: insulin analogs and biosimilars. Insulin analogs are genetically engineered formulations designed to mimic natural insulin's action but with modified absorption rates for better glucose control. Biosimilars are biologic medicines similar to already-approved insulin products, offering comparable efficacy and safety at potentially lower costs. Together, these segments enhance treatment options for diabetes management, contributing to increased accessibility and affordability for patients requiring insulin therapy.</p></p>

<p><p>The human insulin market is categorized into short-acting, intermediate-acting, long-acting, and pre-mix insulin formulations. Short-acting insulin provides rapid glucose control post-meal, while intermediate-acting insulin offers a more gradual effect, suitable for controlling blood sugar levels throughout the day. Long-acting insulin ensures stable blood glucose levels over an extended period, usually with a single daily injection. Pre-mix insulin combines rapid and intermediate-acting insulin, providing flexibility and convenience for patients needing varied insulin coverage throughout the day.</p></p>
